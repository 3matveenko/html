Russian commanders discussed using nuclear arms in Ukraine, says US
Vladimir Putin was not involved in the talks, they told the BBC's US partner. The White House said it had grown "increasingly concerned" about the potential use of nuclear weapons over the last few months. But it stressed the US saw no sign of Russia preparing for such use. That chimes with earlier Western intelligence assessments that Moscow has not been moving its nuclear weapons. Kremlin spokesman Dmitry Peskov accused the West of "deliberately pumping up the topic", although the timing of the high-level Russian military discussions in mid-October is significant. By late September President Putin had escalated his nuclear and anti-Western rhetoric, talking about using all means at his disposal to protect Russia and the occupied Ukrainian lands he had annexed.
